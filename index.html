Publications

A Method for Extracting Sedimentary Outcrops from UAV Oblique Photogrammetry Point Clouds
Chufan Ren, Chaodong Wu *, Yanan Zhang, Cong Lin, Xinyue Niu, Yanan Chu
Sensors, vol. 26, no. 12, 2026.  |  MDPI
📄 Paper 📋 Cite
Abstract & Details

Abstract

Point-cloud analysis of sedimentary outcrops using UAV oblique photogrammetry is a crucial approach to sedimentary system characterization, stratigraphic correlation, and petroleum exploration analog studies. In large-scale field settings, however, outcrops are often scattered and fragmented, vegetation and soil cover is extensive, and class imbalance is pronounced. To address these challenges, we propose SedCSA-Net, an enhanced PointNet++ architecture that integrates collaborative improvements across four dimensions: data augmentation, sampling strategy, feature encoding, and loss optimization. On the Cretaceous Qingshuihe Formation dataset, SedCSA-Net achieves an mIoU of 89.51% and an OA of 96.08%, with attitude measurements deviating by less than 3° from field references.

Key Contributions

  • Constructed a large-scale sedimentary outcrop point-cloud dataset under real field conditions using UAV oblique photogrammetry, preserving realistic acquisition characteristics (shadow-induced voids, density heterogeneity, SfM artifacts).
  • Proposed SedCSA-Net, a domain-knowledge-driven architecture encoding three categories of sedimentary geological priors as learnable features, combined with a cross-scale self-attention mechanism and class-imbalance-aware training.
  • Validated on a spatial extrapolation partition scheme — outperforming DBSCAN, Random Forest, PointNet, and PointNet++ — and enabled automatic bedding-attitude measurement with <3° deviation, establishing a complete UAV-to-geological-characterization workflow.
